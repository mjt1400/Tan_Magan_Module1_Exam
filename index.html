North Korea Attractions

The Kumsusan Palace of the Sun

The Kumsusan Palace of the Sun, formerly the Kumsusan Memorial Palace and also known as the Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il Mausoleum, is a building in Pyongyang's northeast corner that serves as the mausoleum for North Korea's founder, Kim Il-sung, and his son, Kim Jong-il, who were both posthumously designated as Eternal President and Eternal General Secretary. The Kumsusan Assembly Hall, which functioned as Kim Il-official sung's home, was erected in 1976. Kim Jong-il had the structure refurbished and turned into his father's tomb after the older Kim died in 1994. It is estimated to have cost at least $100 million. According to other accounts, the sum might be as high as $900 million. Kim Il-embalmed sung's body is kept in a transparent glass sarcophagus within the palace. His head is propped up on a traditional Korean buckwheat cushion, and his torso is draped in the Workers' Party of Korea's flag. Kim Jong-remains il's are currently on display in a chamber next tohis father's and are arranged in a same manner.

Kim II Sung Square

North Korea's main public plaza, Kim Il Sung Square, is located in the heart of Pyongyang, the capital. The plaza measures 75,000 square meters (807,293 square feet in size, making it the world's 51st biggest public square.Kim Il Sung Square is most known for holding North Korea's military parades, which are often covered by international and domestic media. Mass dances, rallies, and new year's festivities featuring live performances by the Moranbong band, ice sculptures, and fireworks are all hosted on the square during special occasions. It was finished in August 1954, named after the country's founding leader and everlasting President Kim Il Sung, as the country sought to rebuild itself after being substantially decimated by the Korean War, which ravaged the country from 1950 to 1953. Kim Il Sung Square, which is paved with granite titles, can house up to 100,000 people. White dots painted on the ground are exactly aligned in both north-south and east-west directions. This is how troops and entertainers maintain flawless order during military parades attended by tens of thousands of people. You can see both the front and rear of the square from the square's center. Looking out towards the river, you can see the Taedong River and the Juche Tower, which stands towering on the opposite bank. The majestic Grand People's Study House edifice completes the backdrop to the square on the front side. Anyone visiting Pyongyang should pay a visit to Kim Il Sung Square. It won't let you down, especially with its stunning surroundings. The Foreign Language Book Store, which is only a short walk away, has numerous DPRK literature translated into different languages.

Myohyang

Mt. Myohyang is one of North Korea's five most famous mountains, located in the country's hilly northwestern region. Locals refer to it as the "mystery scented mountain." It is well-known for the breathtaking views from its top, which stands at 1909 meters. Several hiking paths surrounding the mountain are surrounded by unspoiled flower fields, adding to the peak's scenic appeal. According to history, this mountain was originally home to King Tangun, the fabled progenitor of the Korean people. Because it is only a day journey from Pyongyang, this mountain has become a popular tourist attraction in the DPRK.

Mansu Hill Grand Monument

In April 1972, the monument was erected to commemorate Kim Il Sung's 60th birthday. Only Kim Il-sung was featured on the monument at the time. Following Kim Jong-death il's in 2011, a similar monument of him was constructed on the north side of Kim Il-sung. The statue was originally clad in gold leaf, but was eventually changed to bronze. Simultaneously, Kim Il-monument sung's was changed to show him at a later age, smiling. The original Mao outfit worn by Kim Il-sung was likewise replaced with a Western-style suit. Kim Jong-monument il's originally had a long coat, but it was quickly replaced with his distinctive parka. A mosaic painting depicting a scene from Mount Paektu,[5] regarded to be the sacred mountain of revolution, is shown behind the center sculptures on a wall of the Korean Revolution Museum building. Two memorials, one on either side of the statues, lead out from the structure and feature sculptures representing various soldiers, laborers, and farmers engaged in anti-Japanese revolutionary battle and socialist revolution. They are 5 meters tall on average, with a lengthy line of human figures painted on them.

Juche Tower

The Juche Tower was built on the occasion of the 70th birthday of President Kim Il Sung, by the River Taedong in 1982 (Juche 71). The tower has a 20-metre-long torch which can be seen glowing throughout the night. The Juche idea is central to North Korea's policies and political framework, stressing the need of self-reliance and self-defense. You can go up the Juche Tower and have a panoramic view of the whole capital city. It is also possible to sit and relax by the steps leading to the tower seeing people get along with their daily lives. Right across from the river the portraits of the Leaders can be seen in Kim Il Sung Square with the impressive Grand People's Study House in the background. The skyline of Pyongyang had some major changes in 2012 and many of the buildings seen on the right-hand side of the square are new. The Ryugyong Hotel has also seen some changes as it was only a few years ago that this gigantic building was not covered in glass resulting in a concrete pyramid looming over the whole city of Pyongyang.
